Workflow

This page exists to prevent “surprise exclusions” and “we booked the wrong thing” outcomes.

What success looks like
  • Endpoints locked before scheduling
  • Cohorts matched to assay constraints
  • Documented run notes and QC exclusions
  • Standardized outputs you can analyze immediately

How projects move through the core

1
Consult

Align the scientific question, primary endpoint, constraints, and feasible paradigms.

2
Feasibility + design alignment

Confirm cohorts, run order, habituation needs, exclusions, and expected data outputs.

3
Schedule via iLab

Requests and approvals occur in iLab. Staff confirms scope, logistics, and readiness.

4
Run + QC + delivery

We run the study (or supervise limited self-service), document deviations, and deliver data.

Non-negotiables

  • Endpoints are locked before booking. If they change mid-run, your study becomes exploratory.
  • Consult required for new assays, new labs, or non-standard constraints.
  • Light-cycle compliance is mandatory for circadian-sensitive work (and “we forgot” doesn’t count).
  • QC exclusions are final and will be documented with reasons.
  • Staff authority during runs applies in full-service and supervised self-service.

Intake checklist (bring this)

  • Hypothesis and primary behavioral endpoint
  • Genotype/strain, age, sex, N per group (target)
  • Handling history and housing conditions
  • Interventions (drug, surgery, viral, diet) with timing
  • Known constraints: light cycle, transport, phenotype severity

Common reasons studies fail

  • Underpowered design or shifting endpoints mid-run
  • Uncontrolled handling/stress differences
  • Ignoring light-cycle effects for circadian-sensitive tasks
  • Strain/vision limitations for spatial tasks
  • Booking equipment before defining the protocol
